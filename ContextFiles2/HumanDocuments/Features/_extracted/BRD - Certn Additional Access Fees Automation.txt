BRD - CERTN ADDITIONAL ACCESS FEES AUTOMATION
Purpose
This document defines the requirements to automate the handling of additional third‑party access fees incurred during provider verification on the Really Global platform. The goal is to eliminate manual intervention, ensure transparency and compliance with prior provider consent, and reliably recover verification costs without degrading provider trust.
This BRD covers ingestion of monthly access‑fee reports from Certn, provider notification with a 24‑hour notice, and automated follow‑up charging via Stripe.
Feature Name
BRD - Certn Additional Access Fees Automation
Summary
Really Global charges providers upfront for standard verification costs (licenses, education, background checks) using a flat global fee. However, some universities, certification boards, or private institutions impose additional record‑access fees that are not known at the time of purchase. These fees are charged by the institution directly to Certn and passed through to Really Global.
Providers explicitly pre‑authorize these potential additional fees during checkout. When Certn later reports these access fees, Really Global must notify the provider with a 24‑hour advance notice and then charge the saved payment method.
Today, this process is handled manually. This feature automates the end‑to‑end workflow.
Problem / Opportunity
Manual handling of access fees is time‑consuming, error‑prone, and unscalable. Each month, Certn provides reports identifying which verifications incurred additional access fees, but there is no automated mechanism to:
Notify affected providers consistently
Enforce the required 24‑hour notice
Reliably charge the correct payment method
Maintain an auditable trail
Automating this process reduces operational risk, ensures policy consistency, and allows Really Global to scale verification without absorbing third‑party costs.
Goals
Fully automate additional verification access‑fee handling
Ensure providers receive a clear 24‑hour advance notice
Charge providers only after the notice period expires
Eliminate manual Stripe charging and email sending
Maintain alignment with Stripe authorization and compliance requirements
Primary Users / Actors
ProvidersCare providers purchasing verification on Really Global
CertnThird‑party verification partner supplying monthly access‑fee reports
Platform (System)Processes reports, sends notifications, and triggers charges
Finance / OperationsMonitors outcomes but does not execute manual steps
Core Outcome / Experience
Providers understand why an additional charge is occurring, recognize it as something they previously approved, receive advance notice, and experience a predictable, professional charge process.
Internally, the system handles ingestion, notification, delay, and charging without human intervention, while maintaining logs and traceability.
Constraints / Guardrails
Providers must have explicitly pre‑authorized additional access fees during checkout
A minimum 24‑hour notice is required before charging
Charges must use the existing saved payment method from the original verification purchase
All charges must be processed through Stripe
The system must distinguish access fees from standard verification fees
The system must tolerate partial or manual report ingestion
Background Context
Certn charges Really Global monthly for both standard verification fees and additional access fees
Access fees are institution‑specific and variable
Certn reports explicitly identify access fees versus standard fees
Providers are informed of this possibility during checkout and agree to it
Really Global already stores Stripe payment credentials using proper authorization flows
High‑Level Flow
Certn provides a monthly report identifying access fees
Report is imported into Really Global (manual upload acceptable)
System matches report rows to provider accounts via email
System sends a 24‑hour notice email via Mailmodo
System waits 24 hours
System automatically charges the provider via Stripe
Charge outcome is logged
Definition of Done
The feature is considered complete when the following conditions are met:
A. Report Ingestion
System supports importing Certn access-fee reports (CSV or equivalent)
Access fees are programmatically distinguished from standard verification fees
Each fee row can be matched to a provider account using email
B. Provider Notification (24-Hour Notice)
A separate notification email is sent for each individual access fee reported by Certn.
Providers may receive multiple emails if multiple access fees apply; the email copy must clearly explain this behavior.
Each email must include:
Institution name (exact source of the access fee)
Clear description of why the institution requires an additional access fee
Total amount that will be charged
Reminder that this fee was pre-authorized during verification checkout
Clear statement that the charge will occur automatically after 24 hours
The notification must not itemize internal processing or payment costs.
Email must be sent using the Mailmodo template titled: “Verification - Additional Charge - 24 hour notice”
C. Charging Logic
Each Certn access fee is charged as a separate Stripe transaction.
The final charge amount must fully recover all third-party and payment-processing costs incurred by Really Global.
Any internal uplift or adjustment applied to recover processing costs must remain an internal calculation and must not be exposed to providers.
Stripe charges must be executed off-session using the previously authorized payment method.
Charge descriptions must reference the institution name and access-fee context.
No charge may occur if the corresponding notification email fails to send.
Each Certn access fee is charged as a separate Stripe transaction.
The final charge amount must be calculated as: Certn access fee + 10% processing surcharge.
The surcharge exists to fully recover Stripe transaction and card-processing costs.
Stripe charges must be executed off-session using the previously authorized payment method.
Charge descriptions must include the institution name and access-fee context.
No charge may occur if the corresponding notification email fails to send.
D. Failure Handling
Failed charges are logged with error details.
Failed emails prevent charge execution.
Duplicate charges are prevented.
E. Audit & Traceability
Each access fee has a clear lifecycle record:
Imported
Notified
Charged or failed

Open Design & Implementation Space
This BRD defines required outcomes, not implementation specifics. Engineering may choose the simplest reliable approach for report ingestion, per-fee notification handling, surcharge calculation, scheduling, and charging, provided all guardrails and behaviors above are met.
