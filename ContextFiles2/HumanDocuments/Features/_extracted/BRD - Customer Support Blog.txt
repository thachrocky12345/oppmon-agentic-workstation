1. Feature Name
BRD - Customer Support Blog 
2. Problem Statement
Really Global does not currently have a true customer support blog or help center.
Today, users who need help understanding how to use the platform, what features mean, or how certain workflows operate have limited options. While a chatbot exists and has been populated with some support content, not all users will use a chatbot, and the platform lacks a clear, browsable, trustworthy source of written customer support information.
This creates three problems:
Users cannot easily find clear answers to common questions about how the platform works
The platform lacks visible signals of maturity, trust, and user support
Search engines and large language models see limited first-party support content, which weakens trust and SEO signals
3. Goal and Intent
The goal of this feature is to create a simple, clean, and highly usable customer support blog that serves as the primary source of general help and platform-related content for Really Global.
This is not an educational mental health content blog.
This blog exists to:
Help users quickly understand how to use the platform
Answer common questions about features, workflows, and policies
Act as a trust signal for users, search engines, and AI systems
The core values of this feature are:
Simplicity
Clarity
Speed
Elegance
The experience should feel calm, professional, and effortless — never busy, flashy, or overwhelming.
4. Inspiration and Design Direction
The primary design inspiration for this feature is Airbnb’s customer support and help center.
This inspiration is intentional.
The Airbnb help center is:
Text-forward
Clean and uncluttered
Fast and responsive
Easy to browse
Focused on getting users answers as quickly as possible
While the design does not need to be an exact copy, the end result should feel very similar in spirit and usability.
This is not about creativity for its own sake. It is about proven usability.
The designer is encouraged to:
Take inspiration from Airbnb’s structure, layout, and navigation patterns
Keep visuals minimal and purposeful
Prioritize readability and speed over decoration
5. Scope
In Scope
A dedicated customer support blog for Really Global
A clean, browsable home page for support content
Individual support articles explaining how to use the platform
Categories and navigation that make it easy to find answers
A content management system that allows ongoing article creation and updates
Explicitly Out of Scope
Educational mental health articles
Thought leadership or marketing content
Heavy imagery, animations, or visual storytelling
Feature promotion or sales content
This blog is about help, not persuasion or entertainment.
6. Content Philosophy
Content in this blog should:
Be written in plain, clear language
Focus on helping users complete tasks or understand features
Avoid jargon wherever possible
Be structured for easy scanning and reading
Examples of content include:
How to book a session
What the verified badge means
How payments work
How provider profiles function
How to use specific platform tools
Hundreds of articles may exist over time.
Depth is good. Flashiness is not.
7. Technology and Platform
WordPress is the preferred content management system for this feature.
The site is hosted on Kinsta, which provides:
A robust WordPress environment
HIPAA-compliant hosting
Strong backup and recovery tools
Separate staging and production environments
If there is a strong reason to consider an alternative approach, it can be discussed as an architectural decision, but the default assumption is WordPress.
8. SEO and Metadata (Future Requirements)
This customer support blog is expected to play an important role in SEO and trust signaling.
Strict SEO requirements will be added, including:
Metadata standards
Proper heading hierarchy (H1, H2, etc.)
Indexability and crawlability best practices
A separate SEO consultant will provide detailed specifications.
Design and structure should anticipate these requirements, even if the full specs are added later.
9. Success Criteria
This feature is considered successful when:
Users can easily find answers to common platform questions
The experience feels clean, simple, and professional
The blog is fast, responsive, and easy to navigate
The platform clearly signals that it supports and cares about its users
The foundation is in place to scale to hundreds of support articles
10. Final Notes
This BRD intentionally gives strong direction without micromanaging execution.
Design decisions should prioritize clarity, simplicity, and proven usability patterns.
If there are questions or alternative ideas, they should be raised early. This feature is foundational and should be done thoughtfully from the start.
