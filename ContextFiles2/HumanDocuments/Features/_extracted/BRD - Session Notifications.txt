BRD - Session Notifications
Purpose
This document defines the requirements for session-related email and SMS notifications for both Clients and Providers on the Really Global platform. The goal is to increase attendance, reduce confusion around refunds and scheduling, and ensure Providers are reliably prepared for sessions.
This BRD groups related notification behavior into a single document for speed. Engineering may split this into separate epics or tasks for implementation.
Feature Name
Session Notifications
Summary
This feature establishes a standardized notification system covering the full lifecycle of a scheduled session, including booking confirmation, refund-policy reminders, session reminders, and schedule changes. Notifications must be clear, minimal, and operationally reliable across time zones.
Problem / Opportunity
There is currently no consistent session notification system for Clients or Providers. This leads to missed sessions, refund confusion, provider uncertainty after changes, and unnecessary support burden. Improving notification reliability directly improves marketplace trust and liquidity.
Goals
Increase session attendance and reduce no-shows
Ensure Clients clearly understand the 24-hour refund and rescheduling policy
Ensure Providers receive timely, low-noise reminders
Eliminate confusion when sessions are canceled or rescheduled
Primary Users / Actors
ClientsIndividuals booking sessions on the platform.
ProvidersIndependent care providers offering sessions.
PlatformResponsible for triggering and delivering notifications.
EngineeringResponsible for implementation and integrations.
Core Outcome / Experience
Clients feel confident they are booked, understand their options, and are reminded at the right moments. Providers trust that they will not miss sessions and are only interrupted when it matters. The system operates automatically without manual intervention.
Constraints / Guardrails
Email is required for all key lifecycle notifications
SMS is reserved for time-sensitive reminders and schedule changes
Refund policy must be communicated at booking and before the refund cutoff
All notifications must be timezone-correct
Notifications must not require manual internal intervention
SMS must support STOP and HELP and honor opt-out globally
Definition of Done
Architecture and Dependencies
Transactional notifications must be sent from a dedicated Twilio sender separate from any marketing traffic
Carrier-level opt-outs must be honored and logged
Client Notifications
General Rule for Late BookingsIf a session is booked after a reminder window has passed, that reminder is skipped. Future reminders that have not yet passed must still be sent.
Booking Confirmation
Email is sent immediately with session details, refund policy, and a calendar invite or add-to-calendar link
SMS is not sent unless the session begins within 12 hours of booking
48 Hours Before Session
Email reminder is sent including refund-policy reminder
SMS reminder is sent including manage-appointment link
24 Hours Before Session
Email reminder is sent
SMS reminder is sent without refund-policy language
1 Hour Before Session
SMS reminder is sent with join link
10 Minutes Before Session
SMS reminder is sent with join link
Provider Notifications
Booking Confirmation
Email is sent with session details and calendar invite
SMS is not sent
24 Hours Before Session
Email reminder is sent
1 Hour Before Session
SMS reminder is sent
10 Minutes Before Session
SMS reminder is sent
Schedule Changes
When a session is rescheduled, all pending reminders for the old time are canceled and a new set is created for the new time.
Client-Initiated Cancellation
Client receives email confirmation
Provider receives email and SMS
Provider-Initiated Cancellation
Client receives email and SMS
Provider receives email confirmation
Dependencies
Background job scheduler with delayed send and cancellation support
Mailmodo for email delivery
Twilio for SMS delivery

Action: Purchase or configure a dedicated phone number (or Messaging Service) for these transactional alerts.

2. Calendar Implementation (.ics)
[ ] Library Selection: Select a library (e.g., ical-generator for Node, icalendar for Python) to generate valid .ics files on the fly.
[ ] Timezone Check: Ensure the .ics file content explicitly defines the start/end times in UTC or includes the correct timezone offset to prevent "floating time" errors in Outlook/Google Calendar.
3. The "Late Booking" Unit Test
Requirement: If I book a session that starts in 2 hours...
[ ] Pass: System sends "Confirmation Email" (with ics).
[ ] Pass: System skips "48h" and "24h" triggers.
[ ] Pass: System DOES schedule/send the "1 Hour" and "10 Minute" SMS reminders.
4. The "Reschedule" Integration Test
Requirement: User moves Friday 2 PM session to Monday 4 PM.
[ ] Pass: Friday 2 PM reminders (48h/24h/1h) are deleted from the job queue.
[ ] Pass: Monday 4 PM reminders are created in the job queue.
[ ] Pass: Client receives "Rescheduled" confirmation email.
[ ] Pass: Provider receives "Rescheduled" SMS + Email.
5. Failure Handling
[ ] Twilio 21610 Error: Ensure the code catches error 21610 (User hit STOP) gracefully and logs it as "Opted Out" rather than retrying endlessly and clogging the error logs.
