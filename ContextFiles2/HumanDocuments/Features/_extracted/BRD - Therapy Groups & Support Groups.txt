BUSINESS REQUIREMENTS DOCUMENT (BRD) – LIGHT
1. Feature Name (Working Title)
Therapy Groups and Support Groups
2. Summary
This feature introduces bookable group sessions on Really Global surfaced as two navigation entry points: Therapy Groups and Support Groups. It enables Providers to publish a scheduled group event with a defined capacity so multiple Clients can book the same time slot and attend together. Therapy Groups are restricted to licensed provider types. Support Groups are available to all provider types. The goal is to expand access and affordability for Clients, unlock a high‑leverage revenue format for Providers, and create new inventory that can scale without requiring 1:1 time for every incremental Client.
3. Problem / Opportunity
Today, the platform’s booking model assumes a 1:1 session: when a Client books a time slot, it becomes unavailable to all other Clients. That makes groups impossible because groups require a single scheduled time block to remain bookable until capacity is reached.
This is worth solving now because group sessions: - Expand access to care via lower per‑person pricing and shared support. - Increase provider earning potential per hour and reduces the need for constant 1:1 availability. - Creates new marketplace inventory types that are highly discoverable and can become a major acquisition surface.
4. Goals
Enable Providers to publish group events that multiple Clients can book until capacity is reached.
Support two clear group categories with appropriate eligibility rules:
Therapy Groups: licensed provider types only.
Support Groups: all provider types.
Make it obvious to Clients what they are booking (group vs 1:1) and what to expect.
Ensure group bookings work end‑to‑end with scheduling, payment, and notifications.
Prevent overbooking, double booking, or broken calendar behavior.
5. Primary Users / Actors
Clients
People who want a lower-cost or community-based option instead of 1:1 sessions.
Providers (Licensed)
Licensed providers who want to run Therapy Groups and/or Support Groups.
Providers (Non‑licensed)
Coaches, mentors, allied providers who want to run Support Groups.
Platform (System)
Enforces eligibility rules, capacity logic, booking/payment, and communications.
Support / Ops
Handles disputes, cancellations, attendance issues, and policy edge cases.
6. Core Outcome / Experience
A Provider can create a group event with a start time, duration, price per attendee, and a maximum number of attendees.
A group event remains bookable until the attendee limit is reached.
A Client can discover group sessions via the top navigation entry points (Therapy Groups and Support Groups) and book a seat.
Clients experience group booking as a first-class product, not a hacked 1:1 appointment.
All attendees receive the same standard communications (confirmation, reminders, changes/cancellation notices), tailored to group context.
The system prevents the “slot disappears after first booking” behavior for group events.
7. Constraints / Guardrails
Eligibility
Therapy Groups can only be created by licensed provider types.
Support Groups can be created by any provider type.
A licensed provider may choose to run a Support Group.
Capacity
Group pricing is assumed to be per attendee.
The platform must support a configurable attendee limit per event, with an upper bound of 50 participants.
Once capacity is reached, the event must become unbookable.
Booking integrity
Group events must never overbook.
Group events must be visually and structurally distinct from 1:1 appointments on the Provider calendar so Providers can immediately tell the difference.
Scheduling
Date, time, duration, and time zone behavior must behave exactly the same as existing session types (video/audio).
Recurring behavior must be supported in the future, but is not required for initial release.
Single-instance group events must still be distinguishable from 1:1 sessions on the calendar.
Communications
All attendees must receive the standard booking lifecycle notifications, treated the same as 1:1 sessions.
Payments
Group bookings must use existing payment, cancellation, and refund logic with no special-case rules.
Visibility
Capacity remaining and sold-out states may exist functionally but do not need to be prominently advertised to Clients in early stages.
8. Success Signals
Providers create and publish group events (adoption).
Group events receive bookings (activation).
Group attendance rate is comparable to or better than 1:1 sessions.
Lower support burden than expected (few disputes around “what I booked,” refunds, or access).
Incremental GMV attributable to group sessions.
9. Risks / Open Questions
Calendar clarity
Ensuring Providers can clearly differentiate group sessions from 1:1 sessions at a glance.
Eligibility enforcement
Confirming that non-licensed providers cannot access or publish Therapy Groups under any circumstance.
Recurrence readiness
Ensuring group events created today will not block or complicate future recurring-session support.
10. Definition of “Done”
Therapy Groups and Support Groups are visible as distinct navigation entry points.
Providers can create group events with a maximum attendee count and price per attendee.
Clients can book a seat in a group event until capacity is reached.
Group events stop accepting bookings immediately when capacity is reached.
Group bookings trigger the correct lifecycle notifications to all attendees and the Provider.
Group events reliably connect attendees to the session experience at the scheduled time.
Eligibility rules are enforced (licensed-only for Therapy Groups).
11. Open Design & Implementation Space
This document defines what must be achieved, not how it must be built.
Engineering, Design, and QA are encouraged to propose solutions that best achieve the outcomes above, including approaches not explicitly anticipated here.
This freedom is intentional.
